## 1. COMT ENZYMATIC INHIBITION EQUATION
In the Pefkon-240mg matrix, Epigallocatechin Gallate (EGCG) from *Camellia sinensis* acts as a potent competitive inhibitor of **Catechol-O-Methyltransferase (COMT)**. This protects the catechol rings of the *Pinus pinaster* monomers from rapid methylation. The functional elimination rate constant ($k_e$) of the active monomers is mathematically modified as a function of EGCG concentration ($[I]$):

$$k_{e, \text{synergistic}} = \frac{k_{e, \text{baseline}}}{1 + \frac{[I]}{K_i}}$$

Where:
*   $k_{e, \text{baseline}}$ = Standard unadjuvanted clearance rate ($0.46\text{ hr}^{-1}$).
*   $[I]$ = Intracellular concentration of bioavailable EGCG molecules.
*   $K_i$ = The inhibition dissociation constant unique to the EGCG-COMT complex.

By driving down $k_e$, the functional half-life of your primary proanthocyanidins increases from **1.5 hours to over 3.3 hours**, significantly expanding the temporal window for endothelial vasodilation and free radical Hydrogen Atom Transfer (HAT) shielding.
